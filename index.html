Østfold university college 30 years!

Exactly 30 years ago, in 1994, Østfold District college, Østfold engineer College, Østfold nursing college, Østfold social work college and Østfold teaching college became what we today know as Østfold university college. The university are eager to celebrate the jubilee.

30 years, as long as one can see

When walking into the college, there is no question that something is going on. Wherever you see there is banners and stickers to inform everyone that the celebration is started.

Great colleagues

Harald Borgebund is a professor at HiØ. He works as an associate professor in political science. -I work as a lecturer for the bachelor study in international communication, while also working with research. Recently, he published an article about the relationship between libertarianism and the welfare state. What do you think about HiØ as a workplace? - The best about HiØ is the great colleagues. Also, I think Halden is such a great place to be! Mr Borgebund also mentions he produces a podcast together with Sondre Lindahl about political science.

Jubilee party for the employees

According to the Great Norwegian Encyclopedia, from the summer of 1994, the Norwegian university college reform was completed. 98 smaller colleges were merged into what became 26 larger colleges. The reform had a goal of creating new, strong and efficient colleges in all of Norway’s counties. For Østfold, this resulted in Østfold university college. The college based in Halden and Fredrikstad can offer a wide range of studies, all of which is divided into three faculties. The studies vary from associate degrees, all the way up to doctorates.

In the course of 30 years, there have been changes.

The facade has undergone a makeover and Chrisitan August upper secondary school has moved out.

The biggest visual difference of HiØ was probably the new college building though.

With it’s modern and seek design, HiØ could open the newly renovated and newly built building in 2006.

Fredrikstad campus might be the one that has undergone the biggest changes.

The campus is a great and modern place of study, built on the remains of the old Fredrikstad mechanical shipyard.
